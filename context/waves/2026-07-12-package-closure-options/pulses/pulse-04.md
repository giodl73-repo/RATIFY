# Pulse 04: Candidate Selection and Role Review

## Selection

Advance two candidates to full scoring packets and comprehension testing:

1. **Progressive primary-balance closure** — tests whether the stronger fiscal
   path is acceptable when marginal closure is concentrated.
2. **Protected partial fiscal path** — tests whether voters prefer lower current
   burden while explicitly accepting higher projected debt.

Retain the 11.5% VAT package as a sensitivity comparator. Do not lead with it:
the required rate is no longer a modest broad-contribution adjustment, and its
price, credit-delivery, cash-flow, and small-business effects need deeper work.

## Role findings

- **Constitutional boundary:** no synthetic result creates federal taxing or
  lawmaking authority; the eventual authority path, judicial review, and rights
  remedies remain mandatory ballot disclosures.
- **Democratic legitimacy:** concentrated burden does not itself prove consent
  or rejection. National vote equality remains primary; geography is reported.
- **Minority rights:** retained credits, continuity, appeals, and enforceable
  rights may not be traded away by an automatic fiscal correction.
- **Election administration:** construction-stage comparison must not appear as
  a three-way binding ballot. Only a fully scored selected challenger advances
  to Yes/No ratification.
- **Simulation audit:** exposure scores reveal assumed patterns only. They must
  never be relabeled household incidence or voter support.

## Decision gate

Pass. A two-candidate construction comparison is intelligible if the same
common package is shown once and each card highlights only its marginal funding,
debt, uncertainty, correction corridor, and affected groups. Final ratification
remains binary.
