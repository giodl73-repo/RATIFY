# Pulse 03: Household, Service, and Regional Stress

## Method boundary

An ordinal exposure model tests whether the closure margins hide burden groups.
Its profile weights and values are synthetic stress inputs, not population
shares, dollar incidence, welfare estimates, or preferences. Common VAT,
emissions, defense, and Medicare effects are excluded because they do not differ
among the three closure margins.

## Result

| Option | Weighted mean | P90 | Highest modeled exposure |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Broader contribution | 49.6 | 65 | Small-business operator, 78 |
| Progressive closure | 20.6 | 90 | Multinational owner/executive, 100 |
| Partial fiscal path | 51.8 | 88 | Young worker, 88 |

The mean is not a ranking rule. It reveals the distribution pattern: broad VAT
exposure, concentrated progressive exposure, or deferred intergenerational
exposure.

## Nonhousehold risks

- Broader contribution: high tax-administration, price/cash-flow, and credit-
  delivery risk; low geographic concentration.
- Progressive closure: high realization, income-shifting, multinational, and
  ownership-response risk; low direct low-income burden.
- Partial path: high future interest, budget-pressure, intergenerational, and
  forecast risk; low near-term administrative disruption.

All require independent household, business, regional, and service analysis.
