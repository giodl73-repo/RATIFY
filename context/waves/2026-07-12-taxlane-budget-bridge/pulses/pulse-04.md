# Pulse 04: Instrument and Ballot Readiness Review

## Correction

Instrument review found that a 5% cap on “verified administration savings” was
not evidence. The generator now caps that category at zero until a reviewed
joint dollar score exists. The challenger was regenerated and remains fiscally
closed.

## Instrument mapping

Mapped the remaining four funding envelopes to explicit CBO option families:
AGI surtax and related progressive instruments; emissions tax or a rescored VAT;
employer-health and itemized-deduction reforms; and specified defense and
Medicare Advantage changes.

## Role review

- Fiscal closure: pass at envelope level.
- Comparative Evidence Steward: pass for traceable individual option ranges;
  blocks arithmetic package claims.
- Deliberation and Information: candidate labels are now concrete enough for
  public research, not for a ballot.
- Constitutional Boundary: blocks final authorization without legislative text
  and legal pathway.
- Minority Rights: blocks without household protections, health access,
  continuity, and appeal provisions.
- Election Administrator: blocks without finalized text, official summary,
  effective dates, litigation closure, and implementation ownership.

## Decision-gate answer

The workflow can construct a fiscally closed, evidence-linked candidate without
calling it consent. It cannot yet construct an honest ratification offer because
the options require variant selection, interaction modeling, legislative
specification, and a joint score.

## Next wave

Open instrument specification and joint-score design. The next result should be
a small number of fully specified variants rather than a larger option catalog.
