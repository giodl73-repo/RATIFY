# Pulse 03: Challenger Construction

## Result

Selected an approximate actual-ballot medoid as the challenger candidate. It is
one complete ballot rather than a fieldwise synthesis.

The selected synthetic candidate chooses the primary-balance path:

- FY2036 adjustment: $981.0B;
- cumulative 2026–2036 primary adjustment: $5.225T;
- first-order FY2036 debt: 107.6% of GDP;
- progressive revenue: 30.8%;
- broad revenue: 16.2%;
- tax-expenditure reform: 22.5%;
- primary-outlay adjustment: 26.0%; and
- verified administrative savings: 4.5%.

## Role findings

- Simulation Auditor: pass for reproducible construction; no behavioral or
  representativeness claim.
- Fiscal-closure gate: pass at category level; every share reconciles.
- Deliberation and Information: blocks ratification because categories such as
  “progressive revenue” are not executable policy instruments.
- Constitutional Boundary: blocks authority claims and requires actual legal
  text and pathway.
- Minority Rights: requires instrument-specific distribution, access,
  continuity, and appeal analysis.
- Future Citizen: upstream commit, hashes, snapshot, method, and output are
  retained and traceable.

## Decision

The candidate is ready for instrument mapping, not for a public Yes/No ballot.
The final example must not replace specific taxes, offsets, benefits, timing,
and legal text with attractive category labels.
