# Pulse 04: Medicare Protections and Readiness Review

## Result

Specified the uniform 10% Medicare Advantage benchmark change and concrete
continuity, network-failure, plan-exit, enrollment, Original Medicare, Medigap,
appeal, monitoring, and scoring rules.

## Readiness finding

The broad/shared package is ready to submit for independent joint scoring. It
is not ready to display a final savings percentage or to go to a binding ballot.

The old component arithmetic is no longer a defensible package total:

- the VAT credit has an unscored cost;
- 40% of net emissions receipts is returned or used for transition;
- the defense option starts later, includes workforce costs, and now names a
  force implementation;
- Medicare starts later and adds beneficiary protections; and
- interactions, implementation, macroeconomic effects, and interest remain
  unscored.

The truthful top line is therefore the chosen mix—73% revenue and 27% primary
outlay—not an achieved dollar or percentage target. Those percentages describe
design intent until a joint score confirms the realized mix.

## Gate decision

Pass for scoring submission. Fail for ballot publication until reviewers return
annual fiscal, distributional, service, legal, and implementation results and
the sponsor closes any gap without unspecified administrative savings.
