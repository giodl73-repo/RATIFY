# Wave: World Systems First Comparison

## Objective

Build a sourced, comparable first-pass map of Switzerland, Uruguay, Ireland,
Italy, Taiwan, the European Union, and U.S. state mechanisms, then choose which
systems deserve depth packets for the U.S. consensus and reauthorization work.

## Allowed scope

- Comparative institution records and source ledger.
- A public synthesis separating observed rules from transfer hypotheses.
- Role review and the first depth-priority decision.
- No claim that a foreign mechanism is constitutionally available in the U.S.
- No public-opinion prediction and no migration of TAXLANE experiments yet.

## Decision gate

Which two or three systems offer the strongest bounded lessons for a U.S.
citizen chamber, and which apparent lessons fail under federalism, scale,
rights, or administration review?

## Innovation loop

- Learning target: whether double-majority rules create genuine breadth or an
  avoidable territorial veto.
- Comparator: Swiss popular/canton majority, national popular majority, and
  U.S. Electoral College weighting.
- Falsifiable next experiment: test equal national support under different
  geographic distributions and measure passage, vote-weight inequality, and
  veto frequency under each rule.
