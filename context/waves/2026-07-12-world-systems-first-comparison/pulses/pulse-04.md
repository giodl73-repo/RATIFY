# Pulse 04: Wave Close

## Completed

- Seven-system first-pass institutional comparison.
- Physical review parliament and stakeholder roles.
- Sourced transfer synthesis and explicit transfer limits.
- Reproducible geographic consensus-rule stress test.
- Public-priority program-renewal design with anti-capture and rights gates.

## Decision-gate answer

Switzerland, Ireland, and U.S. state systems are the first depth priorities.
Taiwan remains the model for a lower-power public response tier. The Swiss
double majority is useful as an adversarial comparator, but the first run shows
that it can convert geography into minority veto power. Electoral College
weighting is not the RATIFY default.

## Retained failures and gaps

- Comparative outcome and distribution research is incomplete.
- Population, turnout, campaign, and behavioral models are not yet present.
- Federal constitutional pathways require a dedicated legal packet.
- No design yet proves resistance to organized money, government-provider
  ecosystems, misinformation, ballot fatigue, or strategic abstention.

## Next wave

Open public-priority reauthorization: define outcome-first ballots, comparable
program evidence records, conflict and funding-flow disclosure, citizen-panel
review, rights and continuity gates, and an initial capture-resistance
experiment.
