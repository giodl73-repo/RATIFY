# Pulse 02: Seven-System Comparison

## Result

Captured first-pass comparable records for Switzerland, Uruguay, Ireland,
Italy, Taiwan, the European Union, and U.S. state systems.

## Evidence status

- Current formal rules are anchored principally in official institutional or
  constitutional sources.
- Swiss counts through 2025 are official.
- Comparative outcome and distribution evidence remains a depth gap.
- Uruguay needs a closer constitutional route and historical-outcome audit.

## Role findings

- Comparative Evidence Steward: pass for first-pass rules; blocks causal
  “works” claims pending outcome research.
- Democratic Legitimacy Reviewer: Swiss double majority is a test candidate,
  not a default; effective individual vote weights must be reported.
- Minority Rights Guardian: no reauthorization mechanism may expose rights or
  continuity-dependent groups to ordinary sunset.
- Innovation Scout: select Switzerland, Ireland, and U.S. states for depth;
  retain Taiwan as the model for a lower-power response tier.

## Decision

Advance to role-reviewed synthesis, with the hybrid transfer hypothesis stated
as falsifiable rather than recommended.
