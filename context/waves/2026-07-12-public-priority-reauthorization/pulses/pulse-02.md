# Pulse 02: Capture and Framing Stress Test

## Result

Implemented a reproducible synthetic experiment with four fiscally closed
packages and six presentation treatments. Twenty runs of 50,000 synthetic
voters were completed per treatment.

## Findings under declared parameters

- Incumbent branding increases status-quo selection relative to the neutral
  outcome-first condition.
- Hidden paid advocacy increases selection of the sponsored package.
- Funding disclosure reduces the sponsored package's modeled share by 6.4
  percentage points relative to identical exposure with hidden funding.
- Weak evidence and ballot overload increase divergence from evidence-only
  utility and modeled regret.

These are designed sensitivities, not empirical effect estimates.

## Role findings

- Deliberation and Information: require outcome-first comparison, visible
  funding, complexity limits, and comprehension testing.
- Democratic Legitimacy: disclosure is necessary but does not equalize access
  to attention, campaign capacity, or agenda qualification.
- Simulation Auditor: pass as a parameter audit; blocks behavioral claims until
  effect sizes come from randomized or observational evidence.
- Minority Rights: aggregate preference quality does not replace rights and
  continuity gates.
- Innovation Scout: the next comparison should test aggregation of complete
  balanced numeric ballots and whether a medoid avoids synthetic free lunches.

## Decision

Advance outcome-first and funding-disclosed interfaces as design requirements,
subject to empirical validation. Retain brand-first presentation as an
adversarial comparator, not the default.
