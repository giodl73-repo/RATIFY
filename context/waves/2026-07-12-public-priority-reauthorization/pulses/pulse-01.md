# Pulse 01: Timing and Ballot Contracts

## Result

Defined scheduled, triggered, and emergency decision clocks; numeric versus
package ballot selection; open proposal qualification; citizen synthesis;
independent review; published exclusions; a ranked shortlist; and final
reform-versus-status-quo consent.

Added fiscal closure as a ballot invariant: policy, funding, offsets, explicit
borrowing, debt service, and forecast-correction rules travel together.

## Role findings

- Democratic Legitimacy: agenda control must be distributed and exclusion
  reasons appealable.
- Deliberation and Information: five substantive options is a ceiling, not a
  target; complexity and reading time must be tested.
- Minority Rights: continuity and rights floors remain outside ordinary numeric
  allocation and majority repeal.
- Election Administrator: stagger review portfolios; emergency action cannot
  wait for a mass ballot.
- Simulation Auditor: median is a candidate for one-dimensional rates, but the
  100-point allocation rule remains unresolved. Fieldwise aggregation is
  blocked because it can synthesize an infeasible package nobody selected.
- Innovation Scout: the next falsifiable comparison should test program-name
  framing and aggregation methods together.

## Decision

Advance to a capture-and-framing experiment with both discrete renewal choices
and numeric priority allocation. Do not treat TAXLANE's arithmetic mean as the
settled civic aggregation rule.
