# Pulse 04: Renewal Wave Close

## Completed

- Outcome-first public-priority renewal design.
- Fiscally closed ballot invariant.
- Scheduled, triggered, and emergency decision clocks.
- Binary final ratification with complete Yes and No baselines.
- Capture and framing parameter-sensitivity experiment.
- Complete balanced-ballot aggregation experiment.

## Aggregation finding

Across 6,000 synthetic balanced ballots, arithmetic mean, feasible geometric
median, and an approximate actual-ballot medoid preserved fiscal closure.
Fieldwise median and fieldwise trimmed mean did not: independently aggregating
uses and funding created gaps and packages nobody selected.

The medoid is especially legible because it is an actual complete ballot, but
it had greater average distance in this run. The geometric median minimized
distance slightly better than the arithmetic mean. No method is yet adopted as
the final civic rule.

## Decision-gate answer

Outcome-first renewal can reduce some incumbent-brand advantage only if agenda
access, evidence, funding disclosure, ballot complexity, and exclusion appeals
are governed. It cannot by itself eliminate organized influence. Essential
continuity and rights remain external gates.
