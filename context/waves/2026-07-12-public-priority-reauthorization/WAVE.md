# Wave: Public-Priority Reauthorization

## Objective

Design and test a system in which citizens set outcome priorities, fiscal
envelopes, and renewal conditions while accountable institutions implement the
technical means under rights, evidence, and audit constraints.

## Allowed scope

- Program evidence and disclosure contract.
- Outcome-first and multi-option ballot contract.
- Citizen-panel selection and response workflow.
- Anti-capture, rights, continuity, and implementation-audit gates.
- Synthetic experiments on framing, organized influence, ballot overload, and
  renewal choices.
- No accusation of corruption without attributable findings.
- No claim that synthetic voters represent public opinion.

## Decision gate

Can outcome-first renewal materially reduce incumbent-provider advantage
without overwhelming voters, destabilizing essential services, or disguising
organized influence?

## Innovation loop

- Learning target: whether separating outcomes from incumbent program names
  changes synthetic renewal choices under equal evidence.
- Comparator: brand-first yes/no reauthorization.
- Falsifiable next experiment: introduce program branding, disclosed and hidden
  financial interests, evidence quality, and ballot complexity; measure choice
  stability and capture sensitivity.
