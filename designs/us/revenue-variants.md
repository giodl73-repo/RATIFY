# Primary-Balance Revenue Variants

Machine specification: `designs/us/revenue-variants.v1.json`.

All three variants pursue the same first-order primary-balance path. They differ
in who carries the revenue side and how much remains for primary-outlay changes.

| Variant | Revenue share | Revenue envelope, 2026–36 | Principal instruments |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Progressive-heavy | 85% | $4.441T | High-threshold AGI surtax, 15% itemized-benefit cap, employer-health exclusion cap, foreign corporate income, capital gains |
| Broad shared-contribution | 73% | $3.814T | Narrow 5% VAT, $25 emissions tax with 2% real growth, employer-health exclusion cap, capital gains |
| Outlay-heavy revenue side | 50% | $2.613T | Narrow 5% VAT, foreign corporate income, capital gains |

## Protections are part of the specification

The broad variants require a refundable per-person VAT credit. The emissions
variant requires a per-capita dividend and regional transition support. The
employer-health exclusion cap requires geography and age-risk adjustment plus a
collective-bargaining transition. Those modifications reduce net revenue or
change behavior and therefore require a new score.

## Why these are not totals

The displayed CBO option estimates cover 2025–2034, generally start earlier,
and were scored individually. The RATIFY variants start in 2027, run against a
February 2026 baseline, add protections, and combine interacting provisions.
Arithmetic proximity to an envelope is only a specification check.

An independent scorer should now be able to identify the intended starting
policy without deciding whether the surtax is broad or high-threshold, which
itemized-deduction limit applies, which VAT base applies, or which emissions
trajectory applies. Material design choices that remain—rebate amount,
transition formula, and coordination language—are explicitly listed as scoring
inputs rather than silently invented.
