# Broad Shared-Contribution Emissions Dividend

Machine specification: `designs/us/broad-shared-emissions-dividend.v1.json`.

The tax begins at $25 per covered metric ton in 2027 and grows 2% annually in
real terms, following one explicit CBO 60962 alternative.

Every dollar of net federal receipts after tax interactions is assigned before
voting:

- 30% quarterly household dividend;
- 10% worker and community transition; and
- 60% primary-balance adjustment.

Adults receive equal shares and qualifying dependents receive half shares. The
dividend uses the VAT-credit eligibility and payment rails, is not taxable, and
does not reduce federal means-tested benefits.

## Transition formula

The transition share is not an undefined grant pool:

- 4% of net receipts for displaced-worker wage, health, and pension bridges;
- 3% for formula-based community tax-base and public-infrastructure transition;
- 2% for low-income energy affordability and efficiency; and
- 1% for tribal, territorial, rural, and energy-isolated communities.

Allocations use verified emissions, employment, revenue, energy-burden, and
displacement data. Contracts publish beneficial ownership and performance.
Receiving transition funding creates no privileged role in RATIFY evidence or
campaigning.

## Fiscal consequence

CBO's published $813.6B estimate does not include this allocation, starts
earlier, and uses a different window. Returning and transitioning 40% of net
receipts means the primary-balance contribution is substantially smaller than
the earlier scale basket assumed. That shortfall must be resolved openly after
joint scoring; it cannot be filled with administrative savings.
