# Primary-Balance Outlay Variants

Machine specification: `designs/us/primary-outlay-variants.v1.json`.

| Variant | Outlay share | Envelope, 2026–36 | Starting instruments |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Progressive-heavy | 15% | $784B | Medicare Advantage benchmarks plus enumerated nondefense discretionary changes |
| Broad shared | 27% | $1.411T | Defense funding path plus Medicare Advantage benchmarks |
| Outlay-heavy | 50% | $2.613T | Defense, Medicare Advantage, Medicaid per-enrollee caps, hospital payments, crop insurance, and enumerated grants |

The outlay-heavy variant demonstrates the real tradeoff. Reaching half the
primary-balance adjustment through spending requires changes large enough to
touch defense capabilities, state Medicaid finance, beneficiary access,
providers, hospitals, farms, and state and local services. It cannot be sold as
eliminating generic waste.

## Floors are binding specifications

Every variant preserves rights, lawful commitments, active-treatment
continuity, and appeal. Defense reductions require a capability and workforce
plan. Medicare Advantage changes require county, network, quality, plan-exit,
and enrollee-transition review. Medicaid caps must respond to enrollment and
economic or public-health shocks and preserve eligibility, benefit, disability,
long-term-care, and provider-access floors.

Those modifications are not free: they can reduce savings and change behavior.
The CBO figures are therefore magnitude references, not estimates of the RATIFY
variants.

## Readiness

The progressive and outlay-heavy variants still contain CBO option families
that need program-level schedules. The broad-shared variant is more compact but
its defense line remains a budget path rather than a strategy. None is ready for
ratification or Voter Lab preference claims until a common joint score and
plain-language impact record exist.
