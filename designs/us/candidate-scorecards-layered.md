# Layered Candidate Comparison

> Package-construction aid—not a vote and not an independent score.

## Layer 1: What changes?

| Question | Progressive primary balance | Protected partial path |
|---|---|---|
| Who carries the added cost? | Higher-income filers and affected multinational stakeholders | Future taxpayers and service users through more debt and interest pressure |
| What is the target? | Primary balance by 2036 | Smaller partial adjustment by 2036 |
| What is projected 2036 debt? | 107.6% of GDP | 113.6% of GDP |

Current-law comparison: 120.2% of GDP. Everything else in the protected policy
package is the same.

## Layer 2: What could automatically change?

- **Progressive:** the added surtax can move only between 2.5 and 3.5 points,
  in annual steps no larger than 0.25 point. Corporate rules cannot change
  automatically.
- **Partial:** no tax or service can change automatically.
- **Both:** anything else requires new legislation and ratification. Rights,
  credits, active-treatment continuity, and earned commitments are outside the
  automatic corridor.

## Layer 3: What is uncertain?

Neither package has an independent score. In the lower synthetic case, the
progressive package is $1.307T short of its stronger target and the partial
package is $112B short of its smaller target. The figures test construction;
they do not predict enacted results.

## Check before choosing a construction candidate

1. Which package places added current burden on higher-income filers and
   multinational stakeholders?
2. Which package accepts 113.6% debt-to-GDP in the retained comparison?
3. Can either package automatically cut protected services? **No.**
4. Has either package received an independent joint score? **No.**

If any answer is unclear, return to the relevant layer before expressing a
construction-stage preference.
