# Broad Shared-Contribution Medicare Advantage Policy

Machine specification: `designs/us/broad-shared-medicare-advantage.v1.json`.

Beginning in 2027, Medicare Advantage benchmarks fall uniformly by 10% in every
county. The reduction is simple enough for a Yes/No ballot, but its effects may
not be hidden behind a federal-savings number.

Plans must preserve active treatment when networks change, arrange
out-of-network care at in-network cost sharing during a material network
failure, and provide expedited independent appeals. Plan exits and serious
network failures trigger neutral counseling and a special election period.
Affected beneficiaries retain access to Original Medicare and receive a
time-limited guaranteed-issue route to available Medigap coverage.

CMS already requires adequate networks and includes continuity protections in
Medicare Advantage rules. This specification makes the package's stronger
protections explicit and requires their cost to appear in the score.

The prior $489 billion scale figure is not this policy's score. Its window,
start date, enrollee responses, and protections differ. Reviewers must estimate
federal savings, beneficiary costs, plan and provider responses, access, and
distribution together for 2027–2036.
