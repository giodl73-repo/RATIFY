# Broad/Shared Closure Options

Machine specification: `designs/us/broad-shared-closure-options.v1.json`.

The package-construction stage now has three honest choices:

| Option | Central synthetic result | Principal tradeoff |
|---|---:|---|
| 11.5% narrow VAT with scaled credit | Exactly reaches primary-balance target | Much larger broad consumption contribution |
| 3-point high-income surtax plus foreign corporate reform | $153B above target | More closure concentrated on high-income filers and multinational stakeholders |
| Keep package, choose partial path | $848B above partial target | 2036 debt around 113.6% rather than 107.6% of GDP in the retained first-order comparison |

These figures are construction sensitivities, not scores. Under the lower
fiscal-effect assumptions, all three remain short of their respective targets.
The purpose of the comparison is to choose what should be formally scored, not
to imply that uncertainty has disappeared.

## Correction rule

The broad option may adjust the VAT only from 10.5% to 12.5%, with its credit
scaling automatically. The progressive option may adjust the surtax only from
2.5 to 3.5 percentage points. Annual steps are bounded. The partial path has no
automatic tax or service change. Anything outside those corridors requires new
legislation and ratification.

The options are compared upstream. After one challenger is selected and fully
scored, the binding public question remains the expected Yes/No choice: adopt
that funded challenger or retain current law and its debt path.
