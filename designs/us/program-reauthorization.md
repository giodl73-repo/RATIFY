# Program Reauthorization Design

The research question is whether voters can periodically review public programs
without creating arbitrary cliffs, ballot overload, or violations of lawful
commitments.

## Candidate sequence

1. An independent evidence office publishes cost, outcomes, beneficiaries,
   distribution, audit findings, and alternative designs.
2. A stratified citizen review panel produces plain-language findings and
   competing arguments.
3. Agencies, legislatures, affected communities, and the public may respond.
4. Voters choose among renewal, reform, corrective status, consolidation,
   gradual sunset, or termination.
5. Courts preserve constitutional rights and vested legal commitments.
6. The legislature implements the result within a defined envelope and reports
   deviations publicly.

Termination should require a higher threshold than ordinary renewal when basic
rights, vulnerable populations, long-term contracts, or system continuity are
at stake.
