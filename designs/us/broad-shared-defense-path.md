# Broad Shared-Contribution Defense Path

Machine specification: `designs/us/broad-shared-defense-path.v1.json`.

This component makes one uncomfortable choice visible. It uses CBO's published
2027–2034 budget-authority reductions and implements them as a proportionally
smaller version of the 2025 force, approaching 17% fewer active-component
personnel and associated units by 2034. It is scoreable starting policy, not a
claim that an across-the-board smaller force is the best defense strategy.

CBO estimated $1.118 trillion less budget authority and $959 billion less in
outlays for 2025–2034. But its estimate starts in 2026, and CBO says the outlay
estimate does not reflect any particular military alternative. RATIFY therefore
does not repeat $959 billion as this package's savings. It starts in fiscal 2027,
uses the published 2027–2034 budget-authority path, and claims nothing outside
that window until a scorer prices the actual force and transition schedule.

## Workforce and capability discipline

The path first uses lower accessions, attrition, and voluntary separation.
Involuntary separation is last, earned benefits remain intact, and transition
costs must be included in the joint score.

The package must publicly test force sufficiency, readiness, deployment tempo,
munitions, logistics, the industrial base, nuclear command and control, cyber,
alliances, current operations, and affected communities. A failed test cannot
be waived and cannot be financed invisibly. The sponsor must revise the force
plan, replace savings, or add revenue before the binding ballot.

This is deliberately more demanding than saying "cut defense by a trillion."
It gives reviewers a falsifiable plan and gives voters the consequences of
repairing it.
