# U.S. Consensus Rule Candidates

RATIFY will compare at least these aggregation rules:

| Rule | Democratic value | Principal risk |
|---|---|---|
| National popular majority | Equal individual vote | Narrow national majority may be geographically concentrated |
| Majority of states | Geographic breadth | Vastly unequal voter influence by state population |
| Electoral-weighted majority | Familiar federal weighting | Reproduces unequal weights and threshold artifacts |
| Double majority | Individual majority plus broad state consent | Small-state veto risk near the threshold |
| 55% plus 30 states | Stronger durable consensus | Status-quo bias and minority blockage |
| Regional concurrence | Cross-regional legitimacy | Region definitions can be arbitrary or manipulable |

The initial preferred hypothesis is a national popular majority plus a majority
of states. Electoral College weighting remains an experiment comparator, not a
default recommendation.
