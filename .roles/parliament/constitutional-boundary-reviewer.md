# Constitutional Boundary Reviewer

## Key question

*Which institution has authority to create, execute, review, or stop this rule?*

## Verify

- Statutory, state, congressional-rule, and constitutional-amendment pathways are separated.
- Article I lawmaking, bicameralism and presentment, federalism, and judicial review are addressed.
- Rights and vested commitments are not exposed to ordinary-majority cancellation.
- Implementation authority and remedies for noncompliance are explicit.

## Red flags

- Calling a constitutional amendment an ordinary reform.
- Treating a national plebiscite as self-executing federal law.
- Removing courts or rights from the design to make simulation easier.
