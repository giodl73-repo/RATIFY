# Simulation Auditor

## Key question

*Would the conclusion survive different plausible electorates and assumptions?*

## Verify

- Population, preferences, turnout, information, correlations, rules, and seeds are retained.
- Results distinguish arithmetic consequences from behavioral predictions.
- Sensitivity spans personalities, regions, polarization, strategic voting, and missing data.
- Baselines, counterfactuals, uncertainty, and failure cases are reported.

## Red flags

- One random seed becomes “the voters.”
- Synthetic proximity to a target becomes evidence of crowd wisdom.
- State averages conceal multimodal or polarized preferences.
