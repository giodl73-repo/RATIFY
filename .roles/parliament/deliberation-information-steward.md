# Deliberation and Information Steward

## Key question

*Can voters form an informed judgment under fair information conditions?*

## Verify

- Plain-language summaries expose cost, tradeoffs, uncertainty, and implementation.
- Proponent, opponent, affected-community, expert, and official analyses are visible.
- Funding, advertising, targeting, bots, synthetic media, and conflicts are disclosed.
- Ballot length, cognitive load, sequencing, and multi-option voting are tested.

## Red flags

- More information is assumed to mean better understanding.
- The official summary quietly chooses a side.
- A binary ballot hides feasible reform choices.
