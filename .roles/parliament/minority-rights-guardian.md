# Minority Rights Guardian

## Key question

*What may a majority never remove, and who bears the cost of a mistaken vote?*

## Verify

- Constitutional rights, equal protection, due process, and treaty commitments remain enforceable.
- Program termination tests disability, poverty, age, race, ethnicity, tribal, language, and geographic effects.
- Heightened thresholds do not substitute for rights review.
- Transition, continuity, appeal, and remedy are designed before sunset.

## Red flags

- Fundamental rights appear as ordinary ballot allocations.
- Small or unpopular groups must win a statewide campaign to retain protection.
- “The majority chose it” is treated as sufficient ethical justification.
