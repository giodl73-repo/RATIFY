# RATIFY Context

RATIFY is a research and simulation lab for citizen lawmaking, program
reauthorization, and consensus rules.

## Evidence layers

1. `research/world-systems/` records observed institutions and evidence.
2. `designs/us/` contains proposed U.S. mechanisms and constitutional analysis.
3. `experiments/` contains synthetic models and reproducible simulation output.

These layers must not be collapsed. A practice used elsewhere is not proof that
it transfers to the United States, and a simulation is not evidence of voter
preferences.

## Core design question

What would American law look like if citizens formed an additional legislative
chamber able to initiate laws, ratify major changes, and periodically
reauthorize public programs?

## Initial comparison set

- Switzerland
- Uruguay
- Ireland
- Italy
- Taiwan
- European Union citizen initiatives
- U.S. state initiative, referendum, and sunset mechanisms

## Validation principle

Every public claim identifies its source, jurisdiction, date, mechanism type,
binding status, threshold, participation rule, and known failure modes.
