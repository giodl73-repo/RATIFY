# Joint Package Score Contract

## Purpose

All RATIFY variants must be estimated as integrated packages against one
baseline, window, implementation calendar, and set of economic assumptions.
Adding isolated option scores is prohibited.

## Required annual fiscal table, 2026–2036

- gross revenue by provision;
- income and payroll tax offsets;
- refundable credits, dividends, and transition payments;
- primary outlay changes by program and provision;
- federal administration and implementation cost;
- interactions and double-count prevention;
- total primary adjustment;
- incremental net interest;
- total deficit effect;
- debt held by the public; and
- debt as a percentage of GDP.

Every value carries central, lower, and upper scenarios or a documented reason
that a range is unavailable.

## Required nonfiscal table

- household net burden by income, age, and family type;
- state and regional effects;
- worker, employer, owner, and industry effects;
- beneficiaries, enrollment, premiums, out-of-pocket costs, benefits, access,
  networks, quality, and plan exits where health provisions apply;
- defense forces, readiness, capabilities, contracts, personnel, communities,
  and allies;
- state Medicaid finance, eligibility, enrollment, benefits, provider rates,
  and alternative coverage;
- emissions, energy prices, dividends, and transition;
- implementation capacity, systems, staffing, litigation, and timing; and
- protected-rights, continuity, and appeal compliance.

## Interaction matrix

The score identifies at least:

- surtax interactions with deductions and employer-health exclusion;
- VAT and emissions-tax price, wage, profit, income-tax, and payroll-tax offsets;
- VAT credits and emissions dividends;
- capital-gains realization and corporate-income responses;
- federal spending reductions and resulting tax or safety-net effects;
- Medicaid changes and marketplace, employer, state, and uncompensated-care effects;
- Medicare Advantage benchmark, risk, enrollment, premium, benefit, and Part B effects;
- defense and nondefense workforce and regional effects; and
- debt changes and interest feedback.

## Uncertainty and correction corridor

The proposal specifies before voting:

1. a central estimate and disclosed uncertainty range;
2. a reserve that is a real funding source, not an assumed efficiency gain;
3. annual forecast-versus-actual public reconciliation;
4. small automatic adjustments allowed inside explicit voter-approved bounds;
5. legislative correction for deviations outside the corridor;
6. early public review for persistent material deviation; and
7. rights and essential-service floors that automatic corrections cannot cross.

## Common comparison basis

All variants use:

- CBO February 2026 current-law baseline;
- fiscal years 2026–2036;
- common nominal GDP and interest assumptions in the conventional comparison;
- an additional macroeconomic-feedback scenario;
- identical treatment of implementation cost and timing;
- identical household units and incidence concepts;
- identical service and rights metrics; and
- current law as a complete scored No case.

## Voter Lab gate

Voter Lab may test interface, framing, and parameter sensitivity before a joint
score. It may not compare substantive package popularity using placeholder
costs or missing impacts. A package-preference run requires a versioned score
record satisfying this contract or an explicit synthetic-only scenario matrix
that varies every missing value across plausible ranges.
