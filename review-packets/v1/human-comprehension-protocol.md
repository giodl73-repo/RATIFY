# Human Comprehension Protocol

## Hypothesis

The persistent symmetric difference strip, common-facts refresh, and optional,
non-gating fact check improve
recovery of who pays, the fiscal target, debt consequence, correction authority,
and unscored status without giving either candidate a material framing advantage.

## Design

- preregister wording, outcomes, exclusions, and analysis before recruitment;
- randomize candidate order and persistent-plus-refresh versus sequential
  layered presentation;
- include neutral, candidate-favorable, and overloaded stress variants;
- recruit at least 400 completed participants for an initial design test;
- report rather than conceal results for low numeracy, limited English
  proficiency, disability and assistive-technology use, high fatigue, and short
  completion time;
- never infer national support or demographic voting behavior from this sample;
- collect open-text explanations to detect confident misunderstanding; and
- make the fact check educational and optional, never a condition of voting.

## Primary outcomes

1. correct identification of principal marginal exposure;
2. correct fiscal target;
3. correct ordering and approximate magnitude of 2036 debt;
4. correct automatic-correction authority;
5. recognition that neither candidate has an independent score; and
6. time, dropout, confidence, and confidence-conditioned error.

## Provisional acceptance criteria

- at least 70% correct on every primary fact overall;
- at least 60% correct on every fact in every reported accessibility or
  information stratum with adequate sample size;
- no more than a 5-point accuracy difference between candidate cards under
  neutral presentation;
- no more than a 5-point change caused by card order;
- fewer than 10% confidently asserting that synthetic figures are official; and
- no material accessibility defect or systematic omission of a protected cost.

Failure triggers revision and retest, not exclusion of participants or tuning
the threshold after results are known.
