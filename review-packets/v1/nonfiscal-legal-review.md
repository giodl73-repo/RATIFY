# Nonfiscal, Legal, and Implementation Review

Review both candidates against the same questions. A fiscal pass does not
override a failure involving authority, rights, access, capability, continuity,
or implementability.

## Legal authority and remedy

- Identify what Congress could enact by ordinary statute today.
- Separately identify any national voter-chamber or self-executing ratification
  feature requiring constitutional amendment because of Article I,
  bicameralism, and presentment.
- Analyze federalism, taxing, spending, delegation, due process, equal
  protection, treaty, tribal, vested-benefit, and judicial-review issues.
- Name the reviewing institution, cause of action, remedy, standing problem,
  and implementation consequence for every material legal risk.
- Treat courts and enforceable rights as constraints, not ballot options.

## Services and affected groups

Return common metrics for Medicare access and continuity; defense capability,
readiness, workforce, contractors, and communities; household energy burden and
transition; tax and credit delivery; and state, local, tribal, territorial,
rural, disability, age, income, and language-access effects.

For every failed or disputed service test, state whether the candidate must be
revised, financed differently, delayed, or rejected. Do not silently lower the
test to preserve fiscal arithmetic.

## Implementation

Provide an annual critical path from enactment through 2036. Each dependency
must name an owner, statutory authority, system or staffing need, appropriation,
lead time, fallback, audit record, appeal, and failure trigger.
